Bridge Graphical Models:
Markov-Decodable Latent Paths

A path-space view of diffusion, flow matching, bridge-based generation, and world-model dynamics: a latent path is useful only if its dynamics can be decoded by the sampler, policy, or planner that must use it.

Tiantian (Crystal) Zhang · Columbia University t.zhang8@columbia.edu homepage Original research Blog

Core thesis

Many continuous-time generative models train on endpoint-conditioned bridges, but generation requires a non-anticipative Markov decoder that only observes the current state and time. Bridge Graphical Models make this compression step explicit by separating endpoint coupling, bridge law, Markov projection, and dynamics representation.

\[ v^\star(x,t)=\mathbb E[U_t\mid X_t=x],\qquad \mathfrak G(Q)=\int_0^1 \mathbb E\,\mathrm{Tr}\,\mathrm{Var}(U_t\mid X_t)\,dt. \]

The Markovization gap is the MMSE / conditional-variance residual after projecting endpoint-conditioned bridge velocity onto the information available to a Markov sampler. It is a process-side diagnostic before neural training.

Why this may connect to causal/world-model research

Causal representation learning asks which latent variables are identifiable and task-relevant. BGM asks a complementary question: once a latent path or representation is chosen, is it dynamically usable by the Markov sampler, policy, or world model that must roll it forward?

Safe interpretation: the gap measures dynamic decodability / Markov sufficiency. It does not by itself prove causal identifiability or world understanding.

Representation-level extension

A possible extension is to measure whether a learned world-model state retains the information needed for prediction, rollout, planning, or control:

\[ \mathfrak G_{\mathrm{WM}}(\phi) = \int \mathbb E\,\mathrm{Tr}\,\mathrm{Var}\! \left(U_t\mid Z_t=\phi(H_t), A_t, t\right)dt. \]

Here \(H_t\) denotes history or hidden environment state, \(A_t\) action, and \(U_t\) a local dynamics target. This is a proposed analogue, not a completed theorem for all world models.

Current evidence

  • Projection identity: the optimal Markov decoder is the conditional expectation of bridge velocity.
  • The Markovization gap is the irreducible floor of the flow-matching regression objective under the chosen bridge.
  • CPU-scale proxy estimators rank bridge/coupling choices before expensive neural training.
  • Pilot experiments on synthetic data, latent image embeddings, CIFAR-10, and Fashion-MNIST show lower proxy gaps aligning with easier downstream Markov compression under fixed compute.

Questions for discussion

  • Can causal/task-relevant representation learning reduce a representation-level Markovization gap?
  • Can dynamic decodability become a sufficiency criterion for world-model states?
  • Can learned couplings or encoders be trained to produce paths that are both identifiable and Markov-decodable?

Scope and limitations

The current paper is a theory-and-diagnostic framework, not a new state-of-the-art generator. The image experiments are pilot-scale; feature-space proxy gaps are useful for design ranking but are not certified high-dimensional population estimates. The world-model extension is a research proposal: it is mathematically natural under an \(L^2\) projection view, but it should be tested with action-conditioned dynamics and causal/task-sufficient representation objectives.
