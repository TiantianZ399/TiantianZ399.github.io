Research philosophy · updated June 30, 2026

My top-down research question is: how can mathematical structure and optimization geometry reveal the algorithms implicit in modern AI systems, and how can those insights be turned into better algorithms and better decisions?

My trajectory began with computer vision, deep learning, and generative models, then moved toward statistical learning theory, advanced algorithms, theoretical computer science, combinatorial optimization, LLMs, portfolio optimization, online learning, and decision-making under uncertainty.

I am especially interested in LLM theory, previously dive deep in ML/DL theory.

Research

Three representative directions

My projects are connected by one principle: use optimization, algebra, probability, and complexity-theoretic intuition to redesign algorithms rather than only analyze them after the fact.

01

Large language models foundations

My public 2025 automata work studies how representation-theoretic structure can support Transformer simulations of richer finite automata, extending shortcut-style analysis through semidirect products, group actions, and Fourier modules.

02

Generative graphical models(learning)

Bridge Graphical Models study diffusion, flow matching, Schrödinger bridges, and field-based samplers through endpoint coupling, bridge law, Markov projection, and the Markovization gap.

Generative Model Research Blog
03

Online learning theory and Optimization

RIPLM studies sleeping experts through rank-induced Plackett–Luce geometry and mirror descent, aiming for rank-faithful and variance-adaptive online algorithms. DDO studies predict-then-optimize learning through regularized FY/SPO+ geometry, smooth support moments, and compressed or geometry-controlled differentiable oracles.

Mathematics should transform algorithms, not only explain them.

I aim to turn mathematical intuition into algorithmic primitives: algebraic decompositions for Transformer computation, Markov-compression diagnostics for generative paths, rank geometry for online learning, and decision-aware surrogates for optimization pipelines.

arXiv · revised May 2025 · Transformer theory and automata

Partial Answer of How Transformers Learn Automata

Tiantian Zhang

A theoretical project motivated by the open questions in Transformers Learn Shortcuts to Automata. The work develops representation-theoretic constructions for simulating richer finite automata with Transformers, using semidirect-product structure, group actions, and Fourier modules to study when automaton dynamics admit efficient parallelizable realizations.

arXiv
Working manuscript · 2026 · generative modeling theory

Bridge Graphical Models: Markov Compression as a Design Principle for Continuous-Time Generative Modeling

Tiantian Zhang

A path-space framework for diffusion models, flow matching, rectified flow, Schrödinger bridges, and field-based samplers. The central object is the Markovization gap: the unexplained L2 velocity action left after projecting endpoint-conditioned bridge velocities onto Markov-measurable vector fields. The gap gives a pre-training diagnostic for whether a chosen coupling and bridge law can be decoded by a non-anticipative Markov sampler.

arXiv · 2025 · online learning theory

Rank-Induced PL Mirror Descent: A Rank-Faithful Second-Order Algorithm for Sleeping Experts

Tiantian Zhang

A mirror-descent algorithm for sleeping experts under partial availability, using a rank-induced Plackett–Luce parameterization. The project studies how online learners can preserve rank structure while adapting to variance in sequential decision problems.

Ongoing · decision-focused optimization

Deep Decision Optimization: Regularized FY/SPO+ Surrogates and Compressed Moment Oracles

Tiantian Zhang · technical feedback from Prof. Adam Elmachtoub

An active predict-then-optimize direction that views SPO+ through Fenchel–Young/support-function geometry, then replaces hard exposed-face subgradients with smooth support moments. Current work studies compressed moment oracles, including PL, soft dynamic programming, Sinkhorn, perturbation pools, and geometry-controlled neural oracles.

arXiv · 2026 · LLM preference optimization

DDO-RM for LLM Preference Optimization: A Minimal Held-Out Benchmark against DPO

Tiantian Zhang, Jierui Zuo, Michael Chen

A reward-guided decision-distribution approach for LLM preference optimization. Each prompt is treated as a finite decision problem over candidate responses, moving beyond purely pairwise fitting toward reward-guided distribution-level updates.
